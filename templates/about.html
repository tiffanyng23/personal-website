I created this website to share some of the coding projects I've worked on and also just a bit about me and my life.

My goal in life is to be a lifelong learner. I've always been very interested in so many topics in the natural and social sciences, particularly topics that integrate both areas. My interests include human biology, nutrition and exercise science, biochemistry, public health, geography, and evolutionary biology. I just really love learning about how humans, nature, and the world interact with eachother. This is what led me to start solo travelling a few years ago, to learn more about myself, other people, and the world. In the past 1.5 years I've solo travelled to Japan, Portugal, and Spain, with an upcoming solo trip planned for Austria, Germany, and Switzerland. I've also started to read again a few years ago. I loved reading when I was a kid, and it was something that I gradually stopped doing over time. Reading truly makes you more empathetic and understanding of others, and can teach you so much about people and the world. I find that its helped me discover more about the way I want to lead my life, as someone who is finding their way in their 20's. Over the past year, I've been reading a mix of memoirs, non-fiction books about the sciences, fantasy, and sci-fi. Feel free to check out my Goodreads to see what I've read recently.

I also enjoy staying active whether it be going on hikes, playing basketball, or lifting weights at the gym (okay I don't always enjoy this, but sometimes I do). Exercise feels like flipping a switch, my energy levels go up drastically once I do it and it completely lifts up my mood. This year, I'm thinking of picking up a new form of exercise to do consistently. I'm not sure in what form yet, but it'll happen.

I started doing more coding projects recently, partly because its an important skillset to have nowadays, but also as a way to apply my creativity. I've always felt that I have a creative mind, and I want to work on harnessing that creativity more. I enjoy backend, but have focused primarily on building fullstack applications since I like how it allows me to bring my ideas into a visual form. The projects I have shared on this page are my Earthquake Tracker Dashboard, Spotify Artist Explore Page, and my NASA Image Repository. Personally, the one I'm most proud of is the Spotify Artist Explore Page since I was able to use pretty open-ended API endpoints (artists, albums, and tracks data) and build something I feel is pretty unique and something that resonates with me.

I'm hoping that this website will continue to evolve, as I build more projects and life experiences.
